## (E₈)₁ WZW model
The Wess-Zumino-Witten conformal field theory based on the Lie group E₈ at level k=1. The unique holomorphic CFT at central charge c=8 (by E₈ lattice classification). This is the starting point of the entire framework.

## Conformal embedding cascade
The chain of conformal embeddings at level 1:
E₈ → SU(3)_fam × E₆ → SU(3)_fam × SO(10) × U(1) → ... → SM gauge group.
All embeddings are at level 1, preserving the central charge c=8 exactly. This cascade is the mechanism by which the SM gauge group emerges.

## Self-modeling / self-referential boundary
The proposal that the de Sitter cosmic horizon is described by a boundary CFT that models itself. Formalized as a subfactor inclusion in the Type II₁ factor algebra. The Jones index of the subfactor selects φ² (Fibonacci anyon), linking the algebraic structure to quantum topology.

## Jones index φ²
The discrete series value 4cos²(π/5) = (3+√5)/2 = φ². Selected by the computational faithfulness criterion from the Jones discrete series {4cos²(π/n) : n ≥ 3}. This is the unique index giving the Fibonacci modular tensor category.

## Fibonacci τ anyon
The unique non-trivial anyon in the Fibonacci MTC. Quantum dimension d_τ = φ. Fusion rule: τ × τ = 1 + τ. Realized physically by (G₂)₁ WZW at representation (0,1). The Higgs field is identified as the τ anyon.

## SU(3)_fam
The "family" SU(3) factor in the first step of the conformal embedding: E₈ → SU(3)_fam × E₆. Three generations arise from the fundamental representation **3** of SU(3)_fam.

## Z = j(i)^{1/3} = 12
The entropic partition function evaluated at the self-dual point τ = i. j(i) = 1728, so Z = 12 exactly. s₀ = ln(12) per Planck cell. Z'(i) = 0 (saddle point). This fixes the microscopic entropy density.

## Tower height n = 15
The exponent in M_W = M_Pl/12^n. n = 15 yields M_W = 79.2 GeV. Algebraic identity: K(G₂) + K(F₄) = 5 + 10 = 15 = h∨(E₈)/2. Not dynamically derived (F3).

## Paper 2 condensates
The (G₂)₆ condensates that break SU(3)_fam and generate mass hierarchies:
- **S** (symmetric tensor): S = r × diag(1, −1/2, −1/2). Breaks SU(3)_fam → SU(2) × U(1).
- **A** (adjoint): A = a × diag(0, 1, −1). Breaks remaining SU(2) → nothing.
Together they produce c-parameters c₃ = c₀+P, c₂ = c₀−P/2+Q, c₁ = c₀−P/2−Q for the RS bulk mass formula.

## Kill conditions
Specific falsifiable conditions that, if triggered, would invalidate core aspects of the theory. Tracked in Reference Core §9 (KC-a through KC-g).

## Effective level K
K = k + h∨, where k is the WZW level and h∨ is the dual Coxeter number. At k=1: K(SU(3)) = 4, K(SU(2)) = 3, K(G₂) = 5, K(F₄) = 10.

## Prediction tiers
- **Tier A:** Zero free parameters beyond M_Pl. Direct predictions from the algebraic structure.
- **Tier B:** Depend on n=15, which is an algebraic identity but not dynamically derived.
- **Tier C:** Partially fitted or depend on parameters that have not been back-solved from first principles.
